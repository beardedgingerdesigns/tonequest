The Author of Tone™

DW
David Wilson September 21, 1952 – March 19, 2022
Founder & Author of Tone Emeritus

David Wilson picked up his first guitar in 1965 and never stopped searching for the sound in his head. That lifelong pursuit — part obsession, part calling — eventually gave rise to The ToneQuest Report, the publication that became his life's work and a beacon for every serious guitarist who ever wondered why some instruments and amplifiers sing while others merely make noise.

In the early months of 1999, David posted a detailed review of an amplifier on one of the earliest guitar forums on the internet. The response was overwhelming. Players who had never met agreed on the same fundamental frustration: the guitar press was dominated by advertising, and the editorial coverage reflected it. Reviews were rarely critical. Recommendations were driven by relationships between publishers and manufacturers. The real story of tone — who built what, why it worked, how to find it for yourself — was nowhere to be found.

David launched The ToneQuest Report in November 1999 with a simple proposition: no advertising, ever. A modest subscription fee would fund everything. The publication would answer only to its readers. That promise has been kept for every one of the 270+ issues published since.

His method was direct and personal. He called builders at their shops. He visited players at their homes. He asked the questions a serious player would ask, not the questions a press kit suggested. When he spoke with Jason Lollar about pickup winding, the conversation lasted four hours and covered topics that had never appeared in print. When he drove to Nashville to meet Larry Cragg — Neil Young's longtime guitar tech — he came prepared with questions that took months to develop.

David's belief was that tone is not a destination but a practice — a continuous refinement of ears, hands, instruments, and understanding. He called himself The Author of Tone not to claim credit for any particular sound, but to describe a vocation: the patient, meticulous work of listening deeply and writing honestly about what he heard.

David Wilson passed away on March 19, 2022, after a brief illness. He was 69. He published his final issue two weeks before he died.

"Mining for tone surely requires a miner's heart."

— David Wilson, The Author of Tone Emeritus

"He never wrote a dishonest word about a piece of gear in his life. Every review was an act of respect — for the builder, for the reader, and for the music."

— Jason Lollar, Lollar Pickups

Publisher's Note

By Liz Medley, Publisher

I joined David at ToneQuest in 2008, initially handling subscriptions and operations. Over fourteen years, I came to understand that what made ToneQuest special was not the access it provided or even the depth of its coverage — though both were genuinely extraordinary — but the spirit in which David approached every conversation. He was not a journalist extracting information. He was a fellow traveler comparing notes.

When David passed, the question was not whether to continue but how. The answer, I believe, is exactly as he established it: carefully, honestly, and always with the reader's experience as the ultimate measure. ToneQuest exists to help players find their sound. That hasn't changed.

We enter our 27th year with the same editorial independence that David fought for in 1999. No advertising. No conflicts. Just the real story of tone.
