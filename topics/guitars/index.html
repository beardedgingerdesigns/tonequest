The Wizardry of Oz Noy — Bending, Time, Tone, and Harmony

Oz Noy is one of those rare modern guitarists whose work dissolves boundaries — where the harmonic language of jazz, the rhythmic weight of groove, and the visceral punch of rock converge into something that defies easy categorization. Born in Israel, trained in the conservatory tradition, and tempered by decades of playing in New York City's most demanding rooms, Noy has built a career that is as musically serious as it is infectiously entertaining.

What sets him apart is not merely technical proficiency — though his command of the instrument is formidable — but a deep, intuitive understanding of how to make a guitar sound conversational. His bends carry weight. His time feel leans and pushes in ways that make a trio sound like a full band. And his tone — warm, cutting, slightly overdriven — sits in a place that recalls both Grant Green and Stevie Ray Vaughan without imitating either.

In this extended conversation, we trace Noy's musical evolution from his earliest influences in Israel to his current work, examining the instruments, amplifiers, and effects that have shaped his sound at every stage.
